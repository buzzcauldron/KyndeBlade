# Kynde Blade: Story & Campaign Design
## 40-Hour Gameplay Structure from Piers Plowman

This document provides the **skeleton** for ~40 hours of story and combat, built from William Langland's *Piers Plowman* (B-text). You can fill in specific dialogue, side quests, and scene details. The structure follows the poem's dream-visions and allegorical figures.

## Protagonist: Will

The player character is **Will**—named after the poem's dreamer. "Will" can mean desire, intention, or the human will. Will is a wanderer seeking truth, caught between the Tower of Truth (heaven) and the Dungeon of False (hell), in a world of folk who have lost their way.

**Party**: Will + 2–3 companions (unlock over Acts 1–3). Companions map to allegorical figures: Conscience, Reason, Patience, Kynde.

---

## Act Structure (8 Acts)

### ACT 1: The Field of Folk (4–5 hours)
*Based on Prologue, Passus 1–2*

**Setting**: Malvern Hills, the Fair Field Full of Folk—a vast plain between the Tower of Truth and the Dungeon of False. Medieval England: laborers, merchants, clergy, knights, beggars.

**Story Beats**:
1. **[OPENING]** Will awakens on the Malvern Hills. A dream? The world feels wrong—folk are idle, corrupt, or desperate. *[You write: opening scene, tone]*
2. **Holy Church** appears. She explains the Tower of Truth and warns of False. She sends Will to seek Truth. *[You write: Holy Church's dialogue, exposition]*
3. Will crosses the Field of Folk. Encounters: beggars, merchants, pilgrims, false hermits. Environmental storytelling: poverty, hypocrisy, hope.
4. **First combat tutorial**—bandits or corrupted folk. Introduce dodge/parry.
5. Will learns of **Lady Mede** (Meed/Bribery) and her planned marriage to **False**. Rumors of corruption at court.
6. **Boss**: Minor antagonist (e.g., False's lieutenant, or a Sin-spawn). Establishes threat.

**Combat**: ~12 encounters. Enemies: Bandits, Corrupted Pilgrims, False's Minions, Wild Beasts.

**Locations**: Malvern Hills, Field of Folk, Holy Church (shrine), Road to Court.

---

### ACT 2: Lady Mede and the Court (4–5 hours)
*Based on Passus 3–4*

**Setting**: Royal court, city, Lady Mede's domain. Corruption, wealth, political intrigue.

**Story Beats**:
1. Will travels to court. **Lady Mede** is to marry **False**—a union that would corrupt the kingdom. *[You write: court intrigue, Mede's character]*
2. The **King** considers wedding Mede to **Conscience** instead. Conscience refuses—he denounces Mede as corrupting.
3. Will allies with **Conscience** and **Reason**. They must convince the King to reject False.
4. Court intrigue: bribes, trials, factions. Will navigates moral choices.
5. **Boss**: False, or Mede's champion. Victory: False is banished; Mede's influence checked.
6. Will wakes from the first dream—or does he? The world has shifted.

**Combat**: ~14 encounters. Enemies: Court Guards (corrupt), Mede's Retinue, False's Agents, Bribed Knights.

**Locations**: Royal Court, City Streets, Mede's Manor, Trial Hall.

---

### ACT 3: The Confession of the Sins (5–6 hours)
*Based on Passus 5–7*

**Setting**: The Field of Folk again, but transformed. Reason preaches; the Seven Deadly Sins confess. Piers Plowman appears.

**Story Beats**:
1. Will sleeps again. **Reason** preaches to the Field of Folk. The people repent—sort of.
2. **Seven Deadly Sins** confess: Pride, Envy, Wrath, Sloth, Greed, Gluttony, Lust. Each Sin is a region or dungeon. *[You write: each Sin's confession, personality]*
3. The penitents seek St. Truth but get lost. **Piers Plowman** appears—honest plowman, guide, Christ-figure. He will help if they help him plow his half-acre.
4. **Piers' Half-Acre**: Will and party labor. Some refuse to work. **Hunger** punishes the idle—a harsh lesson. *[You write: labor, Hunger, moral weight]*
5. **Truth** sends a pardon. A priest challenges it. Piers tears the pardon—grace is not earned by parchment alone.
6. Will resolves to seek **Do-Well** (Dowel). End of Vision 2.

**Combat**: ~18 encounters. Enemies by Sin:
- **Pride**: Arrogant knights, peacocks
- **Envy**: Poisoners, backstabbers
- **Wrath**: Berserkers, soldiers
- **Sloth**: Sluggish beasts, sleep-inducing foes
- **Greed**: Hoarders, thieves
- **Gluttony**: Bloated creatures, tavern brawlers
- **Lust**: Seducers, corrupted desire

**Bosses**: One per Sin (7 mini-bosses) + **Hunger** (major boss).

**Locations**: Confession Grounds, Sin Dungeons (7), Half-Acre, Piers' Farm.

---

### ACT 4: The Search for Do-Well (5–6 hours)
*Based on Passus 8–12*

**Setting**: Will's intellectual and spiritual journey. Meets Thought, Wit, Dame Study, Clergy, Scripture, Imaginatif.

**Story Beats**:
1. Will seeks **Do-Well**. Meets **Thought**—who explains Dowel, Dobet, Dobest. *[You write: philosophical dialogue]*
2. **Wit** and the Castle of Flesh—body and soul, governed by Inwit (conscience). Poverty, marriage, virtue.
3. **Dame Study** scolds Will for ignorance. He proceeds to **Clergy** and **Scripture**—learning, but scholarship alone is not enough.
4. Will dreams within a dream: **Fortune** abandons him. **Kynde** (Nature/God) shows him the world. **Imaginatif** teaches patience and humility.
5. Will begins to understand: Do-Well is active virtue; Do-Better is compassion; Do-Best is spiritual leadership.
6. **Boss**: A manifestation of Will's own pride or despair—internal enemy.

**Combat**: ~14 encounters. Enemies: Scholarly Demons, Pride-Spawn, Doubt, Ignorance (allegorical combat).

**Locations**: Castle of Flesh, Study's Hall, Clergy's Library, Kynde's Wilderness, Dream-Layers.

---

### ACT 5: Charity and the Tree (5–6 hours)
*Based on Passus 13–17*

**Setting**: Feast with Conscience, Haukyn the Active Man, Anima, Tree of Charity. Shift toward Christological allegory.

**Story Beats**:
1. Will feasts with **Conscience**, **Scripture**, **Clergy**, **Patience**. Spiritual and literal food. *[You write: feast scene]*
2. **Haukyn the Active Man**—faith soiled by the Seven Sins. Conscience and Patience teach him penance and poverty.
3. Will meets **Anima** (Spirit). He learns the Church should care for its flock—but often doesn't. *[You write: Church critique]*
4. Will realizes **Piers is Christ**. He must seek **Charity**.
5. **Tree of Charity**—Piers is the gardener. Will witnesses the Fall, the life of Christ. **Faith** (Abraham) and **Hope** (Moses) seek Christ.
6. **Good Samaritan**—Christ as healer. Will learns of salvation and love.

**Combat**: ~16 encounters. Enemies: Haukyn's Demons, Sin-Stained Faith, Church Corruptors, Fallen Angels.

**Bosses**: Haukyn (redeemed or fallen), Tree of Charity Guardian.

**Locations**: Feast Hall, Haukyn's Domain, Anima's Realm, Tree of Charity, Pilgrim Road.

---

### ACT 6: The Crucifixion and Harrowing (5–6 hours)
*Based on Passus 18*

**Setting**: Jerusalem, Crucifixion, Harrowing of Hell. Climactic vision.

**Story Beats**:
1. Will witnesses **Christ/Piers** ride into Jerusalem. The Crucifixion.
2. **Four Daughters of God**—Truth, Justice, Mercy, Peace—debate. Redemption unfolds.
3. **Harrowing of Hell**—Christ descends to free the righteous dead. Will participates or witnesses.
4. **Boss**: Satan or the Devil—final battle of the Harrowing. Victory = redemption.
5. Will wakes. He exhorts his family (or companions) to hear Mass. The world is changed.

**Combat**: ~12 encounters. Enemies: Roman Soldiers, Demons, Lost Souls (redeemable), Hell's Guardians.

**Boss**: Satan / The Devil (Harrowing of Hell).

**Locations**: Jerusalem, Golgotha, Hell's Gates, Harrowing Arena.

---

### ACT 7: Unity Under Attack (4–5 hours)
*Based on Passus 19*

**Setting**: Unity (Holy Church as community). Pentecost. Pride attacks.

**Story Beats**:
1. Will attends Mass. Conscience recounts Christ's Passion and Pentecost.
2. **Piers** (as Peter) is given power by Grace. The Church is unified—briefly.
3. **Pride** attacks Unity. The Church is besieged from within. *[You write: Pride's assault, hypocrisy]*
4. Will must defend Unity or help Conscience rally the faithful.
5. **Boss**: Pride—manifested as corruption within the Church.

**Combat**: ~10 encounters. Enemies: Pride's Minions, Hypocritical Friars, Corrupted Clergy.

**Locations**: Unity Cathedral, Church Grounds, Besieged Villages.

---

### ACT 8: The Antichrist and Conscience's Pilgrimage (4–5 hours)
*Based on Passus 20*

**Setting**: The world in decay. Antichrist. Kynde sends Old Age, Death, Pestilence.

**Story Beats**:
1. Will meets **Need**. He sleeps again—the final dream.
2. **Antichrist** arises. The world falls into chaos. Holy Church is undermined by a hypocritical Friar.
3. **Kynde** sends **Old Age**, **Death**, **Pestilence** to chastise humanity. Will is attacked by Old Age—symbolic of mortality.
4. **Conscience** goes on pilgrimage to seek Piers. He calls on **Grace** for help.
5. **Ending**: Will wakes. The poem ends open—Conscience's pilgrimage continues. *[You write: your ending—hopeful, ambiguous, or dark]*

**Combat**: ~12 encounters. Enemies: Antichrist's Army, Death's Servants, Pestilence, Old Age's Minions.

**Bosses**: Antichrist, Old Age (or Death), Final Boss (your choice).

**Locations**: Ruined World, Antichrist's Domain, Pilgrim's Path, Final Threshold.

## Placeholder Scenes for You to Write

1. **Opening**: Will on Malvern Hills. Tone, voice, first lines.
2. **Holy Church**: Her exposition. How does she speak? Formal? Cryptic?
3. **Lady Mede**: Court intrigue. Mede's personality—seductive or cold?
4. **Each Sin's Confession**: Pride, Envy, Wrath, Sloth, Greed, Gluttony, Lust. One scene each.
5. **Piers' Half-Acre**: Labor, Hunger, moral weight. What does work mean?
6. **Feast with Conscience**: Dialogue, atmosphere.
7. **Harrowing of Hell**: Will's role. Witness or participant?
8. **Ending**: Conscience's pilgrimage. Hope or despair?

- **New Game+**: Harder Sins, alternate endings.
