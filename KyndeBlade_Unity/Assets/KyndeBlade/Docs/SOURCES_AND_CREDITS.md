# Sources and Credits

KyndeBlade’s narrative and dialogue use **Middle English** and an **archaic-modern** register. The following sources are drawn on and should be credited in the game and in any publication.

---

## Primary literary sources

### William Langland, *Piers Plowman* (c. 1370–90)

The game is explicitly based on Langland’s dream-vision poem. Direct borrowings and close adaptations include:

- **Opening:** “In a somer seson, whan softe was the sonne” (Prologue).
- **Fair Field:** “A fayr feeld ful of folke fonde I ther bitwene” (Prologue).
- **Tower:** “A tour on a toft, trieliche ymaked” (Passus II).
- **Concepts:** Wille the dreamer, the Tower of Trewthe, the feeld ful of folke, Do-Wel / Do-Bet / Do-Best, Grace that cometh not, labour and poverte.

**Credit:** *Piers Plowman* by William Langland; Middle English quotations and setting adapted for the game.

**Electronic edition (dialogue authoring, line work):** The University of Michigan **Corpus of Middle English** publishes a searchable *Piers Plowman* witness suitable for writers and implementers aligning beats to the B-tradition text:

- [PPlLan — full text (quod.lib)](https://quod.lib.umich.edu/c/cme/PPlLan?rgn=main;view=fulltext)

Use alongside internal beat maps (e.g. `ProjectArchive/docs/DIALOGUE_SOURCE_TEXT_PLAN.md`). Cite the **scholarly edition** and this URL in credits or patch notes when substantial excerpts are used.

---

### Jos Charles, *feeld* (Milkweed Editions, 2018)

Jos Charles’s poetry collection *feeld* reinvents archaic spelling and second-person address (“feeld,” “folke,” thee/thou) in a contemporary lyric voice. KyndeBlade’s use of **feeld** and **folke** as the standard in-game spellings, and the blend of Middle English–derived vocabulary with a readable, slightly fractured syntax, is indebted to this work.

**Credit:** Jos Charles, *feeld* (Milkweed Editions, 2018). The game’s use of the spellings “feeld” and “folke” and its reinvention of an archaic lyric register are inspired by and pay homage to Charles’s work.

---

### *Sir Gawain and the Green Knight* (late 14th c., anonymous)

The Green Knight’s covenant (strike for strike, a yeer and a day) and the tone of his challenge are drawn from the Middle English romance.

**Credit:** *Sir Gawain and the Green Knight*; Green Knight dialogue tradition adapted for KyndeBlade.

## In-game and promotional use

- **Credits screen / About:** List William Langland (*Piers Plowman*), Jos Charles (*feeld*, Milkweed Editions 2018), and *Sir Gawain and the Green Knight* under “Literary and poetic sources” or “Narrative and language.”
- **Press / Store text:** When describing the game’s language, mention Middle English and “a register inspired by Jos Charles’s *feeld*” and credit accordingly.
- **Patch notes / Dev logs:** When referring to “feeld” or “folke” or the archaic-modern voice, credit Jos Charles and Langland as above.

## Middle English conventions used in KyndeBlade

- **Spelling:** feeld, folke, fayr, trewthe, somer, seson, hier, stede, othirworld, yeer, knyght, synne, hede, elde, etc.
- **Verbs:** -eth (cometh, stondeth, bygynneth), -en (abyden, folwen), past participles (fonde, born, ymaked).
- **Pronouns:** thou/thee/thy; ye for plural; his for neuter where appropriate.
- **Vocabulary:** abyden (await), stondeth (stands), woltow (wilt thou), nil (will not), bitokeneth (signifies), drem (dream), wode (wood/forest), stevene (voice), etc.
