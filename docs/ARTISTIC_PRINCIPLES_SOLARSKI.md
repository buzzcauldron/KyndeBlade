# Artistic Principles for Kynde Blade
## Drawing Basics and Video Game Art (Chris Solarski)

This document integrates principles from **Chris Solarski's *Drawing Basics and Video Game Art: Classic to Cutting-Edge Art Techniques for Winning Video Game Design*** into Kynde Blade's art direction. Solarski bridges classical art theory and game design—the same visual grammar (lines, shapes, volumes, value, color) that built two thousand years of art applies to creating emotional experiences in games.

---

## Core Thesis (Solarski)

> **Video games are not a revolution in art history, but an *evolution*.** The success of game artwork relies on the same visual grammar and classical techniques that have evolved over two thousand years. By going back to square one and studying the basic elements—lines, shapes, volumes, value, and color—you discover how to create a range of emotional experiences using classical theory of depth, composition, gravity, movement, and anatomy.

**Key insight**: It's the **preparatory base mesh stage, not the details**, that is primarily responsible for the viewer-player's emotional experience. Details are merely fine-tuning of a broader concept.

## Visual Grammar: The Five Elements

| Element | Role | Kynde Blade Application |
|---------|------|--------------------------|
| **Lines** | Direction, weight, movement | Curved vs angular pathways; character silhouettes; composition |
| **Shapes** | Emotion, identity | Circle = friendly/safe; Square = stable; Triangle = aggressive |
| **Volumes** | Form, depth | Box, sphere, cylinder as base for sprites and environments |
| **Value** | Light, shadow, mood | High-key (light), low-key (dark), mid-key; five-value scale |
| **Color** | Emotion, contrast | Palette per scene; character/environment harmony |

## Elements of Design (Level 5)

### Frame and Camera

- **Close-up**: Intimate connection with character (portrait, still-life convention)
- **Medium shot**: Balanced relationship between character and environment
- **Long shot**: Commanding view; player attention spread across many elements

**Kynde Blade**: Combat UI uses medium framing; overworld can shift to long shot for scale. Parry/dodge eye indicator uses close-up for tension.

### Camera Angle (Horizon Line)

- **High horizon**: Commanding, empowering (e.g., rooftops in *Assassin's Creed II*)
- **Low horizon**: Vulnerability, scale (e.g., *Shadow of the Colossus*)
- **Middle horizon**: Neutral, static (use when tranquility is desired)
- **Tilted horizon**: Tension, disorientation (e.g., *Uncharted 3* drugged scenes)

**Kynde Blade**: Avoid centering the horizon; use high/low for emotional contrast. Low angle for boss encounters (Hunger, Antichrist).

### Scale

- **Small character**: Vulnerability, exploration
- **Large character**: Power, dominance

**Kynde Blade**: Will vs Colossus-style bosses; scale shifts in dream layers.

### Grouping

- **Rule of three**: More than three on-screen elements (or groups) increases visual noise
- **Grouping**: Reduce noise by grouping by function and color (e.g., *Pikmin*, *Empire: Total War*)
- **Composition lines**: Align elements along lines to dictate visual paths

**Kynde Blade**: Turn order UI groups by function; combat parties kept to 2–4; environment elements aligned along parallax layers.

### Lighting (Value Key)

- **High-key**: Light, positive (e.g., *Journey* desert)
- **Low-key**: Threatening, ominous (e.g., *Alan Wake* night)
- **Mid-key**: Balanced, natural daytime
- **Shadowless**: Safe, surreal (e.g., *Animal Crossing*)

**Kynde Blade**: High-key for Field of Folk, Holy Church; low-key for Dungeon of False, Harrowing of Hell, Antichrist; mid-key for overworld.

### Line and Composition

- **Curved lines**: Gentle, flowing, tranquil (Vermeer's *Diana*)
- **Angular lines**: Aggressive, explosive (Rubens's *Massacre of the Innocents*)
- **Pathways**: Curved roads = gentle driving; angular corners = aggressive turns

**Kynde Blade**: Curved pathways for safe zones (Holy Church, Piers' half-acre); angular for Sin dungeons, combat arenas. Character animations: Will and allies use curved movement; Sins use angular.

### Shapes and Emotion

| Shape | Emotion | Examples |
|-------|---------|----------|
| **Circle** | Friendly, safe, youthful, positive | Hobbits, Mario |
| **Square** | Strength, stability, confidence | Range Rover, guardians |
| **Triangle** | Aggressive, threatening, dynamic | Sauron, villains |

**Kynde Blade**:
- **Will, Piers, Holy Church**: Circular/rounded concepts—seeking, honest, safe
- **Lady Mede, False**: Angular—corruption, deception
- **Seven Sins**: Each Sin has a dominant shape (Pride = triangular; Sloth = sagging, heavy; Gluttony = bloated circles)
- **Environments**: Harmony (character shapes echo environment) vs dissonance (character vulnerable in opposing environment)

### Subverting Conventions

- **Unexpected shapes**: Friendly-looking in hostile world (*BioShock* Gatherers); threatening-looking ally (*The Last Guardian* creature)
- **Gender/role subversion**: Memorable when done well (*Metroid* Samus, *Beyond Good & Evil* Jade)
- **Villain design**: *Portal 2* GLaDOS—graceful curves (Venus) inverted for menace

**Kynde Blade**: Consider subverting Sin expectations (e.g., Lust as subtle, not overt; Pride as internal, not peacock). Piers as plowman—humble shape, divine role.

## Character Design Process (Level 6)

### High Concept First

**Lead question**: *What is the emotional experience we want our audience to feel?*

- Define **high concept** (one paragraph) before any art
- Develop **keywords** (adjectives): Emotion, Color, Shape, Texture, Line, Speed, Size, Opposing concept
- Use keywords for **visual research**—search adjectives, not nouns

### Visual Metaphors

- Turn research images into **visual metaphors** (e.g., dead spider → creepy hairstyle; skyscraper → imposing character)
- Reduce to **black-and-white silhouettes** to mask identity and capture abstract qualities
- Combine disparate references into coherent design

### Character Development Pipeline

1. **Brainstorm** → Keywords, high concept
2. **Mind-map** → Categories radiating from concept; nouns that reflect emotions
3. **Research** → Draw silhouettes of reference images; annotate key features
4. **Thumbnail development** → Combine shapes; personify (head, body, legs); echo primary shapes
5. **Silhouette test** → Block in with single value; check readability at small scale
6. **Final drawing** → Details echo concept; avoid disrupting established form
7. **Model sheet** → Reference for team; annotate fabrics, textures

**Kynde Blade**: Apply to Will, Piers, Holy Church, Lady Mede, each Sin, Hunger. Each character needs a high concept and keyword set before pixel art.

### Good Studio Practice

- **Cross-sections**: Develop whole range of characters/environments in unison, not one at a time
- **Daily presentations**: Refine concepts through quick iterations before polish
- **Visible inspiration**: Hang work on walls; mood boards; never assume everyone envisions the same thing

## Environment Design (Level 7)

### Character/Environment Shapes

- **Harmony**: Character shapes echo environment → unified feeling (e.g., *Journey*—triangular character, triangular landscape)
- **Dissonance**: Character in opposing environment → vulnerability or aggression (e.g., circular character in triangular dungeon)

**Kynde Blade**: Field of Folk = mixed (harmony for pilgrims, dissonance for corrupt); Sin dungeons = shape-matched to Sin.

### Building Construction

- **Imperfection**: Sagging, tilting, nature reclaiming—conveys weight and age
- **Skeletal structure**: Show how buildings stay upright; deform to suggest weight
- **Ground connection**: Base/steps create gradual transition; avoid weightless virtual boxes
- **Inhabited**: External features suggest who lives inside (*Mirror's Edge*—AC units, cables)

**Kynde Blade**: Medieval structures—weathered, moss-covered; Holy Church solid but aged; Lady Mede's manor opulent but corrupt; Sin dungeons warped by their Sin.

### Design Approaches

- **Character-centric**: Design character first; environment echoes character (e.g., *Fable 3* Aurorans)
- **Top-down**: High concept for entire game first; then characters, props, environments

**Kynde Blade**: Top-down—campaign structure from Piers Plowman; character-centric for each Sin and allegorical figure.

### Gameplay Map

- **Pathways**: Curved = gentle; angular = aggressive; straight horizontal/vertical = still, tranquil
- **Fractal design**: Logo, characters, map all echo same concept (e.g., *Gears of War* skull motif)
- **Annotate**: Exits, entrances, districts, landmarks, items, events

**Kynde Blade**: Overworld map as pilgrimage path; dungeon layouts reflect Sin (Pride = ascending towers; Sloth = flat, sprawling).

## Drawing Fundamentals (Levels 1–2)

### Five-Value Scale

- **(0)** White/light
- **(1)** Highlight
- **(2)** Middle values
- **(3)** Core shadow (terminator)
- **(4)** Cast shadow (always darker than core shadow)

**Bounced light**: Away-facing planes receive ambient light—omit at your peril; forms flatten without it.

### Atmospheric Perspective

- **Distance**: Diminishing contrast, softer edges, fewer details
- **Foreground**: Strongest contrasts, sharpest edges, most detail
- **Order of drawing**: Background → foreground

**Kynde Blade**: Parallax layers; distant tiles lighter, less detailed; foreground sprites sharp.

### Hatch Lines

- **Straight lines**: Flat planes (box forms)
- **Rounded hatch**: Spherical/cylindrical forms
- **Contour lighting**: Line weight = light (thin) vs shadow (thick)

## Application Checklist for Kynde Blade

| Principle | Apply To |
|-----------|----------|
| High concept before art | Every character, environment, act |
| Shape = emotion | Will (circle), Sins (per-Sin shape), bosses |
| Value key | Scene lighting (high/mid/low) |
| Harmony vs dissonance | Character/environment pairing |
| Curved vs angular pathways | Overworld, dungeons, combat arenas |
| Grouping (≤3) | UI, turn order, party display |
| Silhouette readability | All sprites at 32×48 px |
| Visual metaphors | Research for Piers, Mede, Sins |
| Imperfection/weight | Buildings, terrain, props |
| Fractal design | Logo, UI, map, characters echo theme |
